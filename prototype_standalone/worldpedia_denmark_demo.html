Recent Political History

Denmark transitioned from a Social Liberal-led government under Lars Løkke Rasmussen (2016–2019) to a Social Democratic minority government under Mette Frederiksen after the 2019 elections. A historic broad coalition across left and right was formed after the November 2022 snap election — an unprecedented centrist unity government bringing together Social Democrats, centre-right Venstre, and the new Moderates party, aimed at tackling energy security and economic challenges post-Ukraine war.

Food & Cuisine

🥪
Smørrebrød
Smørrebrød
Open-faced rye bread sandwiches — Denmark's most iconic food. Topped with pickled herring, cold cuts, eggs, liver pâté or shrimp. An art form with strict topping conventions.
⭐ Iconic
🥐
Wienerbrød
Danish Pastry
Flaky laminated pastry the world calls "Danish." In Denmark there are dozens of varieties — spandauer, kanelsnegle (cinnamon rolls), kringle and more.
⭐ Iconic
🍞
Rugbrød
Dark Rye Bread
Dense sourdough rye bread — the backbone of Danish cuisine. Darker, denser and more nutritious than most European breads. Essential base for smørrebrød.
⭐ Iconic
🍖
Flæskesteg
Crispy Roast Pork
Crispy roast pork with crackling — the quintessential Danish Christmas dinner main course. The crackling (svær) must be perfectly crisp. A point of national pride.
⭐ Iconic
🧇
Æbleskiver
Spherical Pancakes
Spherical pancake-like confections cooked in a special pan. Eaten during Christmas season dusted with powdered sugar and dipped in strawberry jam.
⭐ Iconic
🥓
Stegt Flæsk
med Persillesovs
Fried slices of pork belly with boiled potatoes and parsley sauce — voted Denmark's national dish in 2014 in a public poll.
⭐ National Dish
🌿
New Nordic Cuisine
New Nordic
Culinary movement pioneered by Noma (world's best restaurant multiple times). Celebrates local, seasonal Scandinavian ingredients — foraged herbs, fermented vegetables, Nordic seafood.
🌭
Danish Hotdog
Ristet Pølse
Red sausage sold from iconic orange sausage wagons (pølsevogne) across Danish cities. The purist: squiggly mustard, ketchup, raw onion, crispy onions, remoulade and cucumber.

Tourist Attractions

01
The Little Mermaid (Den Lille Havfrue)
Copenhagen · Capital Region
The iconic bronze statue sitting on a rock in Copenhagen harbour. Inspired by Hans Christian Andersen's fairy tale. Though small, it is Denmark's most photographed landmark.
Free Entry
02
Tivoli Gardens
Copenhagen · Capital Region
One of the world's oldest amusement parks (1843) and major inspiration for Walt Disney. A magical blend of rides, gardens, restaurants and entertainment. Transforms seasonally.
03
Nyhavn
Copenhagen · Capital Region
17th-century waterfront canal lined with brightly coloured townhouses, restaurants and old wooden ships. Hans Christian Andersen lived at nos. 20, 67 and 18.
Free Entry
04
Kronborg Castle (Hamlet's Castle)
Helsingør · Capital Region
Renaissance castle immortalised as Elsinore in Shakespeare's Hamlet. Guards the narrow strait between Denmark and Sweden.
UNESCO World Heritage
05
Legoland Billund
Billund · Southern Denmark
The original Legoland, opened 1968 in the hometown of LEGO. A beloved family theme park where everything is made from LEGO bricks. Attracts millions of visitors annually.
06
Roskilde Cathedral
Roskilde · Zealand
Gothic cathedral and UNESCO World Heritage Site that has been the burial site of Danish kings and queens since the 12th century. Contains 40 royal tombs.
UNESCO World Heritage
07
Skagen (Grenen)
Skagen · North Denmark
The northernmost tip of Denmark where the North Sea and Baltic Sea visibly meet. A mystical, windswept peninsula beloved by Danish artists. The Skagen Painters made this world-famous.
Free Entry
08
Louisiana Museum of Modern Art
Humlebæk · Capital Region
One of Scandinavia's most celebrated art museums perched dramatically on the coast of Øresund. World-class collection in a stunning landscape setting.

History

Full timeline
  • 793
    Viking Age
    Viking Age Begins
    The raid on Lindisfarne marks the start of the Viking Age. Danish Vikings expand across Europe, establishing settlements from Normandy to Russia.
  • 958
    Viking Age
    Harald Bluetooth Unifies Denmark
    Harald Bluetooth unifies the Danish tribes and converts to Christianity, creating the Kingdom of Denmark. The Bluetooth technology is named after him.
  • 1015
    Medieval
    Canute the Great
    Danish King Canute rules over Denmark, England, and Norway — one of the most powerful rulers in Europe. The North Sea Empire briefly makes Denmark a European superpower.
  • 1397
    Medieval
    Kalmar Union
    Denmark leads the Kalmar Union — a personal union uniting Denmark, Norway, and Sweden under one monarch. Denmark is the dominant Scandinavian power.
  • 1658
    Early Modern
    Treaty of Roskilde
    Sweden defeats Denmark in the Second Northern War. Denmark cedes its southern Swedish provinces — permanently shrinking Danish territory and reshaping Scandinavian politics.
  • 1849
    Modern
    Constitutional Monarchy Established
    Denmark peacefully transitions from absolute monarchy to constitutional monarchy with the June Constitution, establishing parliamentary government and civil liberties.
  • 1940
    WWII
    Nazi Occupation
    Germany occupies Denmark on April 9, 1940. Unlike most occupied nations, the Danish government initially cooperates — but resistance grows.
  • 1943
    WWII
    Rescue of Danish Jews
    In an extraordinary act of courage, ordinary Danes organize to rescue virtually all of Denmark's 7,000 Jews by smuggling them by boat to neutral Sweden overnight.
  • 2000
    Modern
    Øresund Bridge Opens
    The bridge connecting Copenhagen with Malmö, Sweden opens — linking Scandinavia to continental Europe and transforming the Copenhagen-Malmö region into a single metropolitan area.
